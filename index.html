Hi, welcome to my website! I am Seyeon, Ph.D. Candidate in Macro Organizational Behavior at Stanford Graduate School of Business.

I am originally from Daegu, South Korea. Prior to Stanford, I received a master's from the University of Oxford and a bachelor's from the University of Pennsylvania, both in Sociology.

I will be on the 2025–26 academic job market.

My research is driven by questions at the intersection of entrepreneurship and labor markets. Startups are celebrated for they contributions to economic growth, yet most startups face steep challenges due to limited resources and uncertain futures. My dissertation takes this dual reality seriously and aims to advance our understanding of why, when, and how these challenges arise – both across the startup hiring pipeline, from attraction to recruitment, and in the career trajectories of individuals with entrepreneurial experience.

I am presenting my job market paper at the upcoming AOM Annual Meeting in Copenhagen. The session will take place on Sunday, July 27, from 8:30 – 10:00 AM at the Bella Center (Hall B-B3-m7).
